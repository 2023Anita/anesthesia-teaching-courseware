Program focus

Many beginner resources assume the learner already understands either the operating room or AI tooling. This site starts earlier. It gives students a clear first map: what to notice in anesthesia, how clinical reasoning is communicated, and how AI can help structure learning materials.

The setting is intentionally personal and hands-on: a Cornell first-year learning experience in China, guided by clinical teaching, bilingual explanation, and compact AI-assisted courseware.

How the Materials Are Made

The workflow is intentionally practical: clinical context first, AI as a production assistant, and human review before teaching use.

1. Start from a real teaching need

Each module begins with an actual case or observation day, then turns it into a learner-friendly roadmap.

2. Make concepts visible

Medical infographics explain anatomy, workflow, monitoring, and risk. Hand-drawn illustrations help beginners remember the idea.

3. Keep it easy to share

Standalone HTML pages embed the images, so learners can open them from a link without installing software.

Purpose and Boundaries

This project supports a short, beginner-friendly learning experience for Cornell students visiting China. It is not a clinical protocol library.

These materials are introductory teaching aids. They do not provide patient-specific medical advice, drug dosing protocols, or clinical directives. They do not replace hospital policies, anesthesia records, attending physician judgment, or formal medical training.
