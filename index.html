Introductory anesthesia courseware for visual learning.

These bilingual HTML modules were created for an early medical learner: a first-year Cornell University student interested in anesthesiology. The goal is practical and modest: make the first look into the operating room less abstract, more visual, and easier to discuss.

3 published teaching modules
34 course figures and visual explainers
2 languages: Chinese and English

Why this exists

Many beginner resources in anesthesia assume the learner already understands the operating room. These modules start earlier. They use short explanations, clinical roadmaps, medical infographics, and gentle visual metaphors to help students ask better questions during observation.

The work combines clinical teaching goals with AI-assisted writing and illustration. It is not meant to be a final authority. It is a living set of teaching materials that can improve with feedback.

Course Modules

Each module opens directly in the browser. The standalone versions embed all images, so they are suitable for GitHub Pages hosting and simple sharing.

How the Materials Are Made

The workflow is intentionally practical. Clinical context comes first; AI helps with structure, visuals, and packaging; final use still depends on human review.

1. Start from a real teaching need

Each module begins with an actual case or observation day, then turns it into a learner-friendly roadmap.

2. Make concepts visible

Medical infographics explain anatomy, workflow, monitoring, and risk. Hand-drawn illustrations help beginners remember the idea.

3. Keep it easy to share

Standalone HTML pages embed the images, so learners can open them from a link without installing software.

Purpose and Boundaries

This project is for learning, discussion, and visibility around practical anesthesia education. It is not a clinical protocol library.

These materials are introductory teaching aids. They do not provide patient-specific medical advice, drug dosing protocols, or clinical directives. They do not replace hospital policies, anesthesia records, attending physician judgment, or formal medical training.
Created as a clinician-led, AI-assisted teaching project for early learners interested in anesthesiology. Feedback and learning-oriented contributions are welcome.
